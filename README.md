## What Makes a Good Cheap Hong Kong VPS?

Here's the thing about "cheap" VPS hosting: the price tag is easy to read. What's harder to read is what you're actually getting for it.

The three things that separate a genuinely good cheap Hong Kong VPS from a false economy are:

### 1. Routing Quality

Not all Hong Kong VPS are equal when it comes to how traffic actually reaches mainland China. There are roughly three tiers of routing you'll encounter:

- **CN2 GIA (Premium)**: China Telecom's high-end backbone. Bidirectional premium routing, lowest latency, congestion-resistant. The gold standard — and priced accordingly.
- **Eyeball / CMI + NTT**: A middle ground. Traffic from China Unicom and Mobile often gets direct routing in; outbound uses NTT or similar international backbones. Fast enough for most use cases, meaningfully cheaper than full CN2 GIA.
- **Tier 1 / International**: Standard international routing. Great for global audiences; not optimized for China access. Perfect if your users aren't in the mainland.

Knowing which one you need — and paying only for what you need — is where the real savings happen.

### 2. Hardware & Virtualization

KVM-based VPS with dedicated vCPU and SSD (ideally NVMe) is the baseline worth accepting. Avoid providers that oversell RAM or share CPU in ways that cause random performance spikes.

### 3. Network Bandwidth and Traffic Caps

A 10Gbps port sounds amazing until you realize you only get 100GB of traffic per month. Read the fine print on outbound traffic limits, port speeds, and what happens when you exceed the cap.
